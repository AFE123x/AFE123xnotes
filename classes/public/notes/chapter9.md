# 9 - organizing speech

## how to organize

1. consider your pupose
2. take your audience into account
3. Develop 3 main points (2 - 5)
4. Develop supporting points (2 for each main point)
    - research, data/statistics, examples ...
5. Arrangement - patterns of organization, best way to present
6. Transitions
7. internal previews
8. internal summaries

## examples of arrangement

- spatial: adjacent to one another in location or geography
- chronological: time-based sequence, from beginning to end
- Causal: help audience the link between specific events and their outcome. Cause and Effect relations
- comparison: organize around major similarities or differences between two events, objects or situations.

## signal

- you're moving but connecting the points.

## signposts

help audience understand structure
