# Chapter 11: outlining speech

## what are the two stages of an outline?

- Working outline: all the elements:
    - full sentences, work cited, etc.
    - it shouldn't be a manuscript.
- Speaking outline: short, brief phrases, key words.
    - basically, what you would put on a small card to help guide you throughout the actually presentation.

## outlining your introduction

- your introduction should have the following:
    - attention getter.
    - thesis statement.
    - relevence of topic for audience.
    - speaker's credibility.
    - preview of main points.

## outlining your conclusion

- they should have the 2 components:
    - summary of main point.
    - clincher

## internal previews and summaries

- an internal preview provides a short list that follows.
- an internal summary is a quick review of what happened.

## create speaking outine, elements

- brief: condensed version of working outline with all points, subpoints and source quotationn.
- elements:
    - main point
    - subpoint and sub-subpoints
    - abbreviations
    - evidence
    - difficult words.
    - transitions
    - delivery notes: LOOK UP, SLOW DOWN, EMPHASIS, PAUSE.

## non verbasl delivery skills

- eye contact
- gestures
- physical movement
- proxemics
- personal appearance
